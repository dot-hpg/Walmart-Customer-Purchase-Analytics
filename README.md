# Walmart Customer Purchase Analytics

## Business Problem

Walmart wanted to understand customer purchasing behavior and identify the factors that drive higher spending. The objective was to analyze customer demographics, purchasing patterns, and product preferences to uncover actionable insights that could improve customer targeting, marketing effectiveness, and revenue growth.

The analysis focused on understanding how factors such as Gender, Age, Marital Status, Occupation, City Category, and Product Category influence customer purchase behavior.

## Key Insights

### 1. Male Customers Spend More

Male customers exhibit higher average purchase values than female customers, indicating stronger spending behavior and larger transaction sizes.

### 2. Older Customers Generate Higher Purchase Value

Customers aged 51–55 demonstrate the highest average purchase value, making them one of Walmart's most valuable customer segments.

### 3. Marital Status Has Limited Impact

Married and unmarried customers show nearly identical spending patterns, suggesting marital status is not a major driver of purchase behavior.

### 4. City Category C Leads Spending

Customers from City Category C generate the highest average purchase value compared to Categories A and B.

### 5. Product Category 10 Drives Revenue

Product Category 10 records the highest average purchase value, making it a key revenue-generating category.

---

## Business Recommendations

### 1. Focus on High-Value Customer Segments

Develop personalized marketing campaigns targeting male customers and high-spending age groups.

### 2. Increase Investment in City Category C

Allocate additional marketing resources and promotional campaigns toward City Category C where customer spending is highest.

### 3. Prioritize High-Performing Product Categories

Improve inventory planning, promotions, and cross-selling initiatives around Product Category 10 and other top-performing categories.
